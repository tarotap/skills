# Spread Reference

When to use each spread and what every position asks. Smaller is better —
only reach for a big spread when the question genuinely has many layers.

## Single card

One card, one focused message. Best for daily guidance, quick check-ins, and
yes/no leanings (read the card's overall energy as the leaning; upright tilts
yes, reversed tilts no, but let the card's nature override a mechanical rule —
The Sun reversed is still warmer than Ten of Swords upright).

## Three-card spreads

The workhorse. Same mechanics, different position lenses — pick the lens that
matches the question:

**Past → Present → Future** (things unfolding over time)
1. Past — the root or momentum that led here
2. Present — the current energy, what's actually happening
3. Future — where the current is heading if nothing changes

**Situation → Obstacle → Advice** (what do I do about…)
1. Situation — the honest shape of where things stand
2. Obstacle — what's actually in the way (often not what the user thinks)
3. Advice — the most constructive available move

**You → The other → The relationship** (love and interpersonal)
1. You — your energy, stance, or blind spot in this bond
2. The other — their energy as it enters the relationship
3. The relationship — the field between you; where it's heading

**Path A → Path B → What you're not seeing** (crossroads)
1. Path A — the experience of choosing the first option
2. Path B — the experience of choosing the second
3. What you're not seeing — the hidden factor that should weigh on the choice

**Keep → Release → Learn** (growth and life chapters)
1. Keep — what serves you and deserves protecting
2. Release — what has finished its work in your life
3. Learn — the skill or truth this chapter is asking you to take in

## Five-card spreads

**Timeline** (life direction over a long arc)
1. Distant past — the deep root or old pattern
2. Recent past — what just moved through
3. Present — the pivot you're standing on
4. Near future — the next chapter's opening energy
5. Far future — the trajectory's horizon if the current holds

**Cross** (complex situations, many forces)
1. Theme — the heart of the matter
2. Obstacle — the force working against it
3. Root — what feeds the situation from below
4. Advice — the wisest available response
5. Likely direction — where things trend if the advice lands

## Celtic Cross (10 cards)

Reserve for genuinely layered questions — a life decision, a long-standing
pattern, a situation with several actors. Interpret in this order:

1. **Present** — the querent's current situation and state of mind
2. **Challenge** — the crossing force; the immediate tension (read even a
   "positive" card here as a complication)
3. **Root** — the unconscious foundation; what's really driving this
4. **Recent past** — what is passing out of influence
5. **Crown** — the best that can be achieved; the conscious goal or ideal
6. **Near future** — the next development entering the picture
7. **The querent** — their self-perception and stance toward the situation
8. **Environment** — outside people, pressures, and atmospheres
9. **Hopes and fears** — often both at once; name the ambivalence
10. **Outcome** — the likely resolution on the current trajectory; weigh it
    against card 5 (the possible) and card 6 (the imminent)

Reading tips: cards 1-2 are the engine of the whole spread — get their
tension right first. Compare 5 vs 10 (aspiration vs trajectory) and 7 vs 8
(inner stance vs outer field) explicitly in the synthesis.
